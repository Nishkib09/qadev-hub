⏱️ Why 9 Weeks — The Reasoning
✅
The advantage they already have: As an existing agent, they know the product, the CRM, the parent types, the common scenarios, and the call structure. They don't need the 15-day agent onboarding. That saves 3 weeks right away.
🧠
The mindset shift takes time: Going from doing to evaluating is not instant. An agent who was scored last month must learn to score fairly — including scoring calls that are better or worse than their own. That objectivity is built over weeks, not days.
📊
Calibration is the proof: The final measure of readiness is not whether they can fill in a scorecard — it's whether their scores align within 5% of the Q&T Lead's. That alignment requires repeated practice, feedback, and iteration.
⚠️
9 weeks is the minimum: You could rush it to 6 weeks. The risk: a QA who scores inconsistently undermines the entire quality system. One unfair evaluation can destroy agent trust. Build the foundation properly.

🔄 The Biggest Transition — Agent Brain to QA Brain

This is the hardest part of the entire programme. It's not technical. It's psychological. An agent who becomes a QA must fundamentally change how they relate to their own colleagues, their own performance history, and their own personal call style.

✓ QA Assessor Mindset
Evaluates against the scorecard — not against how they personally would have handled the call
Separates person from performance when scoring and giving feedback
Is equally rigorous scoring a close colleague as scoring someone they don't know
Documents what they actually heard — not what they think the agent meant
Treats their own scores as provisional until calibrated with the Lead
Welcomes disagreement with their score as a learning opportunity
✗ Agent Mindset Carried Over
"I would have handled it differently" — scores based on personal style not the standard
Inflating scores for friends or popular agents to avoid conflict
Being harsher on agents they personally find difficult
Giving vague feedback like "improve tone" without specific examples
Defending a score instead of reviewing it when challenged
Treating the scorecard as a formality rather than a precision instrument
Phase 0
Starting Point Assessment
Before a single call is evaluated, you need to know whether this agent is ready to shadow — and where the gaps are that the programme must close.

✅ What They Already Bring (Agent Advantage)

Gaps — The Programme Closes These

📋 Gate 0 — Readiness Criteria (Must Meet All)
  • ✓
    Minimum 3 months as a live agent at Tap2eat. They must have enough call experience to evaluate interactions credibly.
    Evidence: employment record + Team Lead confirmation
  • ✓
    Personal QA average ≥ 85% over the last 6 weeks. A candidate who is still developing their own call quality cannot credibly evaluate others.
    Evidence: their last 6 weeks of QA scorecard data pulled by the Lead
  • ✓
    No active performance improvement plan (PIP) or unresolved disciplinary process at time of shadowing.
    Evidence: HR check with People Team
  • ✓
    CRM accuracy consistently ≥ 95% over the last month. A QA who audits CRM records must themselves be a strong CRM practitioner.
    Evidence: CRM accuracy data from QA evaluations
  • ✓
    Team Lead endorsement. The direct Team Lead confirms the agent is reliable, professional, and ready to be trusted with colleague evaluation data.
    Evidence: short written endorsement from Team Lead to Q&T Lead
  • ✓
    Written expression of intent. The agent submits a short (1-page) written statement explaining why they want to move into QA and what they believe they need to learn. This tests self-awareness.
    Reviewed by Q&T Lead as part of readiness conversation
  • ✓
    Readiness conversation with Q&T Lead. 30-minute structured conversation covering: their motivations, their understanding of the QA role, any peer relationship concerns, and whether they understand the objectivity requirement.
    Q&T Lead completes readiness assessment form after this conversation

✅ Gate 0 Outcome — Cleared to Start
If all criteria are met: programme begins Week 1.
Q&T Lead issues a written shadow plan to the agent, the Team Lead, and the Technical Operations Manager. The agent is formally in a development shadowing arrangement — not yet a QA Assessor in role or title.
✗ Gate 0 Not Cleared — What Happens
If criteria are not met: a development plan is built instead.
The Q&T Lead creates a specific 4-week plan addressing the gaps (e.g. bringing QA average above 85%, completing a CRM accuracy improvement plan). The shadow programme is scheduled for after that plan is complete. This is not a rejection — it is the honest starting point.
Phase 1
Observe — See Before You Do
The shadow sits alongside the Q&T Lead for the full QA workflow. They don't score yet — they watch, they ask questions, and they build the mental map of how quality evaluation actually works from the other side.
📅 Weeks 1–2 · 10 working days
⚠️
No scoring in Phase 1. The temptation will be to jump straight to evaluating. Resist it. Observing first prevents the shadow from locking in early bad habits before they understand the full scoring logic. Observation is active — they are completing observation logs after every session.

Phase 2
Guided Practice — Score, Compare, Learn
The shadow now scores calls independently before seeing the Lead's score. Every divergence is a teaching moment. By the end of this phase, their scores must consistently land within 10% of the Lead's.
📅 Weeks 3–5 · 15 working days
🎯
The core exercise of Phase 2: Shadow scores the call. Lead scores the same call. They reveal independently. Any gap over 10 points is discussed until both understand the correct interpretation. This is how calibration habits are built. Repeat it 30+ times over 3 weeks.

Score 3 more calls independently

Shadow CRM audit session — begin participating

Score 2 calls independently

Week 3 calibration review — all scored calls this week

✅ Gate 1 — Clearing Criteria
Average scoring divergence <10% across the 5 calibration calls
PLUS: Scorecard interpretation test ≥ 8/10  |  Coaching notes rated "meets standard" or above on at least 2 of 3 submitted  |  No individual call scored with divergence >20%
⏳ If Gate 1 is Not Cleared
One-week targeted extension — specific gaps only
The Lead identifies the exact calibration dimension causing the gap (e.g. consistently over-scoring empathy, or under-scoring CRM accuracy) and builds a 5-day focused exercise plan targeting just that dimension. Then re-attempt Gate 1. Maximum one extension before a programme review is required.
Phase 3
Independent Practice — Run It, Be Checked
The shadow now runs evaluations solo. The Lead does not sit with them. Instead, the Lead spot-checks 30% of all evaluations completed and provides written feedback. The shadow also delivers their first real coaching conversation — to an actual agent, with the Lead present.
📅 Weeks 6–7 · 10 working days
🚀
This is the biggest step in the programme. Up to this point the shadow has always had the Lead nearby. Phase 3 removes that safety net — they must make scoring decisions independently and stand behind them. The Lead's spot-check is a quality control, not a lesson anymore.
📅 Week 6 — First Solo Evaluations
Monday
Phase 3 brief: expectations, submission process, feedback loop
Score 2 calls solo — submit scorecards + notes to Lead by EOD
Tuesday
Lead returns feedback on Monday's scorecards (written, not live)
Score 3 more calls independently
Begin building week's evaluation tracker (own template)
Wednesday
Score 3 calls — include 1 complex/multi-issue call
Write full coaching notes for all 3 (no preview from Lead)
Thursday
Deliver first REAL coaching session — to an agent with low score
Lead observes silently. Gives feedback to shadow after session.
Shadow completes coaching log and files it
Friday
Week 6 debrief with Lead — verbal + written feedback
Shadow: how did the coaching session feel? What would they do differently?
💬
On the coaching conversation: This is where many QA shadows struggle most. They know the scorecard. They know the score. But sitting across from a colleague and saying "here's where you fell short" — with kindness, evidence, and structure — is a different skill entirely. The Lead's post-session feedback focuses 100% on this.
📅 Week 7 — Full Evaluation Week Solo + Gate 2
Monday
Run full solo evaluation day — 4 calls across different agents
CRM audit: 5 records checked independently
Submit all work to Lead by EOD
Tuesday
Lead spot-checks 30% of this week's scored calls
Shadow delivers 2nd coaching session — Lead observes
Shadow begins drafting a mini weekly summary (one paragraph per agent)
Wednesday
Gate 2 calibration: 5 calls scored independently
Lead scores same 5 calls independently
Reveal and compare — divergence must be <8% avg
Thursday
Gate 2 coaching assessment: Lead rates 2 coaching sessions observed
Shadow submits week's evaluation tracker and mini report draft
Friday
Gate 2 results communicated
Phase 3 close debrief — honest two-way review
Brief for Phase 4 if gate cleared
✅ Gate 2 — Clearing Criteria
Scoring divergence <8% average across 5 Gate 2 calibration calls
PLUS: Both coaching sessions rated "meets standard" or above by Lead  |  Evaluation tracker complete and accurate  |  No critical CRM audit errors in the 5 records checked  |  Agent receiving coaching confirmed their understanding of feedback
Phase 4
Final Assessment — The Full QA Week
The shadow runs a complete, unsupervised QA week. The Lead observes remotely, reviews all submissions, and only steps in if something is critically wrong. At the end of 2 weeks, the Lead makes the final certification decision.
📅 Weeks 8–9 · 10 working days
🎯 What "Full QA Week" Means in Phase 4
  • ✓
    Evaluate a minimum of 4 agents per week — minimum 3 interactions per agent — across at least 2 channels
    Target: 12–15 total scored interactions per week
  • ✓
    Complete coaching notes for every scored interaction below 90%
    Notes must be specific, timestamped, and signed off before delivered to agent
  • ✓
    Deliver at least 2 coaching sessions independently — Lead observes one
    Agent must sign coaching log after each session
  • ✓
    Run a mini CRM audit — 8 records checked, findings documented
    Lead spot-checks 3 of the 8
  • ✓
    Complete a draft weekly QA summary report in the standard template
    Lead reviews and scores the report for structure, accuracy, and insight quality
  • ✓
    Participate in a final calibration session — scoring 5 calls against the Lead
    This is the Gate 3 calibration test. Divergence must be ≤5%

🏅 Certification Outcomes — Three Possible Results
✅ Outcome 1 — Full Certification
All Gate 3 criteria met. Shadow is certified as QA Assessor.
Q&T Lead issues written certification to Technical Operations Manager, CX Manager, and People Team. The shadow transitions into the QA Assessor role. If the role is budgeted and approved, this certification supports a formal appointment. If not yet, the shadow remains in an acting QA capacity with documentation supporting the business case for the hire.
⏳ Outcome 2 — Conditional Certification
Mostly meets criteria — one specific dimension below standard.
Shadow is certified with a 30-day conditional period. The specific gap (e.g. report writing, coaching delivery) is addressed through a focused development plan running alongside the QA duties. Formally reviewed at the 30-day mark. No further programme extension needed unless another gate dimension drops below standard.
✗ Outcome 3 — Not Yet Certified
2 or more Gate 3 dimensions not met.
Shadow returns to Phase 3 for a further 2 weeks with a highly targeted plan. This is not a failure of the person — it is a signal that more repetition is needed. The Q&T Lead and the shadow agree on the specific plan together. The agent returns to their call centre duties in parallel. A third attempt is possible but requires a full programme review meeting with leadership.

📌
This tab is for both the shadow AND the Q&T Lead. The programme only works if both parties are honest, consistent, and committed to what the role actually requires. Read both sides.
For the Shadow Candidate
✓ Do This
Ask questions about every score you're unsure of — especially in Phase 1 and 2. There are no dumb questions in calibration.
Score calls using only what you actually heard — not what you assumed the agent meant.
Tell the Lead honestly when you find scoring a friend's call difficult. That's a professional conversation worth having early.
Read your observation logs back before each debrief — come prepared with specific points, not vague impressions.
Accept that your scores will be wrong sometimes in Phase 2. That's the entire point of Phase 2.
Document everything — your reasoning, your divergence, your coaching session notes. Build the habit now.
Continue performing well in your agent role during the programme. Your call quality is still being evaluated.
Be honest in your self-assessments. Overrating yourself in a readiness check delays real development.
✗ Don't Do This
Don't tell colleagues what scores they received or share any evaluation data from the programme. Confidentiality is non-negotiable from day one.
Don't score a call higher because you know the agent is going through something difficult personally.
Don't adjust your score after seeing the Lead's — log your original score first, always.
Don't rush evaluations to hit a number. 3 accurate evaluations are worth more than 6 careless ones.
Don't use "I would have done it differently" as the basis for a low score if the agent met the scorecard criteria.
Don't skip submitting observation logs or treat them as a formality. They are how the Lead tracks your thinking development.
Don't expect to be fully ready in 4 weeks. The 9-week structure exists for a reason.
For the Q&T Lead Running the Programme
✓ Do This
Debrief every divergence with curiosity, not correction. Ask "what did you hear?" before explaining why the score was different.
Give written feedback on submitted scorecards consistently — not just verbal. The shadow needs a trail of their development.
Be honest at gate assessments. A shadow who isn't ready should not move forward — a kind delay protects the quality system.
Protect the shadow's time during the programme — especially in Phase 3 and 4 when they're running evaluations alongside their agent duties.
Keep agent teams informed at the right level — they should know evaluations are happening, not who is doing them during the shadow phase.
Track the programme formally and share progress updates with the Technical Operations Manager monthly.
Celebrate progress openly — the shadow is investing real effort. Recognition is part of good people development.
✗ Don't Do This
Don't use the shadow to reduce your own evaluation load in Phase 1 and 2. They are not a resource yet — they are a learner.
Don't pass a gate to be kind. A shadow who clears Gate 2 without truly meeting the standard will struggle badly in Phase 4.
Don't let the shadow deliver coaching notes to agents without your review until Gate 2 is cleared. Unsupervised feedback at this stage carries reputational risk.
Don't allow the shadow to evaluate their closest friends or people they have a known conflict with during the programme.
Don't skip the debrief sessions, even in busy weeks. They are the learning engine of the entire programme.
Don't allow the programme to drift beyond 9 weeks without a formal review. Indefinite shadowing with no clear endpoint demotivates the candidate.
🔥 When It Gets Hard — Common Friction Points
🧑‍🤝‍🧑
"I have to score my friend's call and it was bad." This happens. The shadow should flag it to the Lead before scoring, not after. The Lead can either score it themselves or sit in for the coaching conversation. This is not weakness — it is professional self-awareness.
💔
"The agent I coached was upset with my feedback." Expected. This is not failure. The Lead debriefs with the shadow after every difficult coaching session. Delivering hard feedback kindly is a skill that takes practice. One upset agent is part of learning — a pattern of upset agents is a signal to revisit coaching technique.
📉
"My own call quality is dipping since starting the programme." This is a real risk. Splitting attention between two roles is demanding. If the shadow's personal QA average drops below 80% during the programme, a check-in meeting with the Lead is required. The programme may need to be paused until their agent performance stabilises.
🤐
"Other agents are asking me about their scores." The shadow must be clear and consistent: "I can't share that." If pressure is applied, they escalate to the Q&T Lead immediately. Confidentiality is not negotiable and any breach is grounds for removing the shadow from the programme.
🌱
"I feel like I'm not improving fast enough." Show them the divergence data. Progress in calibration is measurable. If the data shows improvement week-on-week — even small improvement — that is real evidence to hold onto. The Q&T Lead's job is to make the progress visible, not just felt.
🥬
A Note on What This Programme Really Is
This isn't a training course. It's a gradual transfer of trust. By the end of 9 weeks, the Q&T Lead is saying: "I trust this person to evaluate their colleagues fairly, to tell an agent the truth with kindness, and to represent quality standards I would stand behind." That is the bar. Everything in this plan is designed to get someone to that bar — starting from a strong agent foundation, and building the objectivity, precision, and people skills that a QA Assessor must carry every single day.
