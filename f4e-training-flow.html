Inbound Call Flow (The 5 Stages)

C Inbound Call Handling — End to End
Stage 1 — Opening & Greeting
Pick up on or before the 3rd ring. Identify yourself and the programme.
Standard Greeting
Agent: "Good morning/afternoon, thank you for calling Food4Education Tap2Eat. My name is [Name]. How may I help you today?"
Swahili: "Habari ya asubuhi/mchana, asante kwa kupiga simu Food4Education Tap2Eat. Jina langu ni [Name]. Nikusaidie vipi leo?"
QA
Scorecard Alignment: QA scores the greeting on: answering speed, programme name stated, agent name given, warm tone. Missing any of these = points deducted.
↓
Stage 2 — Account Verification
Confirm the caller's identity. Ask for the registered phone number.
Verification Script
Agent: "To assist you better, may I have the phone number registered on your Tap2Eat account?"
Caller: "0712 345 678"
Agent: "Thank you. Let me pull up your account — one moment please."
!
Never skip verification. A caller who cannot provide the registered number must be directed to visit a school rep or use an alternate verification method. Do NOT access or discuss account details without confirming identity.
↓
Stage 3 — Probing & Issue Identification
Ask targeted questions to fully understand the issue before offering a solution.
Q
Effective Probing Questions:

"Can you tell me more about what happened?" | "When did you first notice this?" | "Is this the first time or has it happened before?" | "What outcome are you hoping for today?"

X
Common mistake: Jumping to a solution before fully understanding the question. If a parent says "my child didn't eat," probe: Was the child present? Did they refuse the food or was it not served? Was there a tag issue? This distinguishes a food quality complaint from a tag malfunction.
↓
Stage 4 — Resolution or Escalation
Resolve what you can at Tier 1. Escalate what you cannot — to the right team.
Resolution Confirmation
Agent: "Based on what you've shared, here's what I can do for you today: [explain action]. Does that address your concern?"
Escalation Handoff
Agent: "I'll need to involve our [Team Name] to resolve this fully. I've logged all the details and they'll reach you within [timeframe]. Your reference number is [ref]. Is there anything else I can help with in the meantime?"
↓
Stage 5 — Closure & CRM Logging
Confirm satisfaction, thank the caller, and log everything in the CRM.
Standard Closure
Agent: "Is there anything else I can help you with today? Thank you for calling Food4Education Tap2Eat. Have a great day."
CR
CRM Entry Checklist (complete before the next call):

Account number · Caller name · Issue category · Resolution or escalation team · Reference number · Notes in clear, specific language. Never write "helped parent" — write what you did.

Warm Sign-off
"Asante. Tupo hapa ukihitaji msaada."
Response Time
First response within 3 minutes. If research needed, send a holding message: "Ninaangalia akaunti yako, subiri kidogo."
Language & Tone
Conversational Swahili or English — not stiff. Use the customer's name. No ALL CAPS. No more than 3 messages without a question to the customer.
X
SMS-specific mistakes to avoid:

1. Sending duplicate messages. 2. Closing with "Asante kwa kuelewa" (presumptuous). 3. Copy-pasting the same template for every query. 4. Ending the chat without checking for additional questions. 5. Using overly formal Swahili that is hard to read on a phone screen.

4

Common Query Types & How to Handle Each

Tag Query
Tag replacement, new tag issuance, lost tag reporting. Check: is the child still at the same school? Escalate to PDA / Area Lead if tag stock is low.
Account Query
Account status, transfer child between schools, transfer funds between accounts, add child to existing account. May need Tech Support for system changes.
Payment Query
Paybill inquiry, reconciliation — "I paid but my balance didn't update." Collect M-Pesa confirmation code. Escalate to Tech Support for reconciliation.
Registration Query
New parent wants to register, add a child to existing account. Guide them through the process. Escalate to PDA for registration forms if needed.
Complaint
Food quality, food portion, learner did not feed. This is the highest-sensitivity category. Acknowledge, empathise, document, escalate to Kitchen Ops / KM.
Feedback / Suggestion
General feedback or improvement ideas. Thank the customer, document clearly, and route to Customer Experience. No escalation urgency unless safety-related.
CR
CRM Category Mapping: Every interaction must be tagged with the correct category in the CRM. This feeds reporting, trend analysis, and resource allocation. Misclassifying a Complaint as an Inquiry means it won't show up in the complaints dashboard — and leadership won't know there's a problem.

Escalation Matrix — Who Handles What

E Escalation Routes & Urgency Classification
Critical — 12 hours
Health or safety risk. Meals not served. Learner affected today. Issue likely to escalate publicly. Notify Team Lead immediately
High — 24–48 hours
Repeated complaints. Parent dissatisfied and expects prompt resolution. Multiple children affected.
Medium — 3–5 days
Valid concern but no immediate risk. Isolated incident or clarification required.
Low — Informational
General feedback, suggestion, or inquiry. No direct impact on service delivery.

!
When in doubt, escalate. It is better to escalate something that could have been handled at Tier 1 than to keep something at Tier 1 that should have gone higher. If you're unsure, ask your Team Lead before closing the interaction.

Quick Reference: Do's & Don'ts

DO

Greet warmly — state your name and programme
Verify account before discussing details
Probe before you prescribe a solution
Use the customer's name naturally
Confirm understanding: "Have I addressed your concern?"
Log every interaction in the CRM immediately
Escalate when unsure — ask your Team Lead
Keep SMS messages short and conversational
Use simple, natural Swahili in chats
Check for additional questions before closing

DON'T

Skip the greeting or sound rushed
Access an account without verification
Answer before you fully understand the question
Use "Asante kwa kuelewa" — it's dismissive
Write vague CRM notes like "helped parent"
Send duplicate SMS messages
Close a chat without a warm sign-off
Mark everything as Critical — use the criteria
Escalate to the wrong department
Leave a call without confirming next steps

A Day as a Call Centre Agent

7:45 AM
Log in to CRM and phone system. Review any overnight escalations. Check Team Lead handover notes.
8:00 AM
Lines open. Handle inbound calls following the 5-stage flow. Log each interaction as you go.
10:00 AM
Check SMS/WhatsApp queue. Respond to pending chats. Ensure no chat is older than 5 minutes.
12:30 PM
Lunch break (30 min). Ensure handover of any open cases to a colleague or note in CRM.
1:00 PM
Afternoon shift. Handle calls, chats. Process outbound follow-ups from the morning's escalated cases.
3:30 PM
Review your CRM entries for the day. Ensure all interactions are categorised and closed correctly.
4:00 PM
Submit end-of-day summary to Team Lead: calls handled, escalations raised, any flags. Log out.
R
Rhythm tip: After every 3 calls, take 60 seconds to review your CRM entries. This prevents backlog and ensures accuracy. Do not wait until end of day to log everything — you will forget details.
