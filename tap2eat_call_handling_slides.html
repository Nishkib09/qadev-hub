The Opening — First 15 Seconds

✅ GOOD — Scored 5/5
"Good morning! Thank you for calling Tap2Eat. My name is Nancy — how may I assist you today?" 😊

✔ Time-of-day greeting
✔ Programme name stated
✔ Agent name given
✔ Offer to assist
✔ Warm, confident tone

❌ BAD — Scored 1/5
"Hello? … Yes, speak. What do you want?"

✗ No time-of-day greeting
✗ No programme name
✗ No agent name
✗ No warmth — sounds annoyed
✗ Parent already feels unwelcome

Empathy & Tone — The Difference It Makes

A real interaction — parent reporting that their child missed 3 days of meals

Parent
My child has not eaten for three days now! I have money on the account — what is happening?!
Agent (Bad)
That's not our problem. The school handles the food. Call them.
Agent (Good)
I completely understand how worrying that must be — let me look into this for you right now. Can I have your registered phone number so I can pull up your account? 🥬
⭐ The good response scored 9/9 on empathy. The bad response triggered a Critical Compliance Failure.

Hold & Transfer — The Right Way

1
Ask permission first
"May I place you on a brief hold while I check that?" — never hold without asking.
2
Give an estimated time
"This should take about one minute." — updates the parent and sets expectations.
3
Warm transfer only
Brief the next person before passing the call. "I have Mr. Mwangi on the line regarding a school transfer — he's been verified."
4
Never hold to manage AHT
Putting a parent on hold because your timer is running high is a QA failure. Quality over speed — always.

The Close — Lasting Impression
