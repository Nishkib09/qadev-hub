💡 Key Insights
1
Tag management is the #1 parent pain point. 278 tag replacements in 21 days signals a parent education gap — many parents don't know how to care for or recover lost tags. A proactive communication campaign can reduce this volume.
2
Onboarding demand is still high. 128 new parent registrations in a single month shows the programme continues to grow. An efficient, scalable onboarding process is essential.
3
Monday visit spikes are predictable. The peak day (Monday, March 9 — 99 visits) aligns with the start of the school week. School reps can staff proactively for this pattern.
4
92% school-level resolution validates the rep model. Only 2% of issues went unresolved, proving that school reps are effective first-line support.
5
Geographic concentration provides focus. Kiambu + Nairobi = 63% of all visits. Other counties (Kakamega, Mombasa, Kisumu, Uasin Gishu, Kajiado) need proportional rep capacity for Term 2 scale-up.
📌 Recommendations & Term 2 Actions
1
Tag Care Campaign — Posters + WhatsApp guides for parents on tag care and replacement process. Target: 30% reduction in tag replacement queries.
2
Parent Onboarding Fast Lane — Monday priority slots with a scripted 5-minute walkthrough for new parent registrations at high-volume schools.
3
Balance Check Self-Service — USSD or WhatsApp balance inquiry tool to reduce low-touch rep visits for simple account queries.
4
Targeted SMS/WhatsApp Nudges — Proactive tips to parents at high-volume schools before common issues escalate.
5
Scale Data Tracking — Roll out Kobo Collect (or integrated in-app tracking) to all schools in Term 2, capturing per-school and per-rep resolution data for campaign targeting.
