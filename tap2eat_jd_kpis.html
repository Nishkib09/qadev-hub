📝 Job Summary

The Quality Assessor is responsible for monitoring, evaluating, and scoring customer interactions across all channels — including inbound and outbound calls, WhatsApp, SMS, and email — within the Tap2eat call centre. Working under the Quality & Training Lead, the Quality Assessor ensures every customer interaction meets the programme's service standards, provides structured coaching feedback to agents, and surfaces data-driven insights to support continuous improvement in customer experience, CRM data integrity, and operational efficiency.

⚙️ Roles & Responsibilities
  • 1 Monitor and evaluate agent interactions on a daily basis across calls, WhatsApp, SMS, and email using the approved QA scorecard, ensuring consistent and fair assessment of all agents.
  • 2 Score agent interactions against established quality standards measuring consistency, professionalism, empathy, issue resolution, and adherence to Tap2eat service protocols.
  • 3 Track First Call Resolution (FCR) rates by reviewing repeat contacts and escalation patterns, identifying root causes and flagging systemic issues to the Quality & Training Lead.
  • 4 Monitor Average Handle Time (AHT) per agent to identify outliers, inefficiencies, or agents who may require additional coaching on call flow or FAQ navigation.
  • 5 Conduct weekly CRM data audits to validate parent and school contact details, flag duplicate or incomplete records, and ensure all interactions are logged accurately and promptly.
  • 6 Deliver structured, constructive feedback to agents through regular one-on-one coaching sessions; document sessions and track improvement actions to closure.
  • 7 Participate in calibration sessions with the Quality & Training Lead and CX Manager to maintain consistency in scoring interpretations and alignment with organisational standards.
  • 8 Identify patterns in agent errors or knowledge gaps and escalate recurring themes for inclusion in the next training cycle or quick-reference SOP updates.
  • 9 Prepare and submit accurate weekly and monthly QA performance reports per agent — covering QA scores, FCR performance, AHT trends, and CRM accuracy — to the Quality & Training Lead.
  • 10 Support new agent onboarding by shadowing and scoring nesting-period calls, providing feedback against the onboarding quality rubric and reporting readiness assessments to the Lead.
  • 11 Maintain and update QA documentation including scorecards, coaching logs, calibration notes, and dispute records in line with departmental standards.
  • 12 Flag urgent quality risks — including complaints, compliance concerns, or data breaches observed during monitoring — to the Quality & Training Lead immediately for escalation.

📊 Quality Assessor — KPI Framework
# KPI Target Description Actions to Own It Ownership
1 Quality Assurance Score ≥ 95% Measures the proportion of evaluated interactions that meet or exceed the QA scorecard benchmark across all channels (calls, WhatsApp, SMS, email). Score minimum 5 interactions per agent per week; ensure scorecards are completed same-day; escalate disputes within 24 hours. Primary Owner
2 First Call Resolution (FCR) ≥ 95% Percentage of parent/school contacts resolved on the first interaction without requiring a follow-up, callback, or escalation. Track repeat contacts per agent weekly; flag unresolved patterns to the Lead; coach on escalation decision-making. Primary Owner
3 Average Handle Time (AHT) ≤ 180 sec Average duration of a customer interaction from initiation to wrap-up. Measures operational efficiency while maintaining quality and issue resolution. Flag agents >20% above target for coaching; review call recordings to identify unnecessary holds, dead air, or navigation inefficiencies. Primary Owner
4 Call Monitoring Coverage ≥ 80% Percentage of agents who have received at least the minimum required number of scored evaluations in a given week. Ensures comprehensive, consistent oversight. Maintain a weekly evaluation tracker; prioritise agents on PIP or with recent complaints; report any gaps to the Lead by Friday. Primary Owner
5 Coaching Session Completion 100% Every agent who received a QA score below 90% in the review period must receive a documented coaching session within 5 working days of the evaluation. Maintain a coaching log; issue signed feedback forms; track action items to completion and document in the QA system. Primary Owner
6 CRM Data Accuracy ≥ 98% Percentage of CRM records reviewed that are complete, accurate, and free from duplication. Ensures parent communication and programme reporting rest on reliable data. Score CRM accuracy as a line item on every interaction scorecard; run weekly spot-check audits; flag inaccurate records to the agent same-day. Supporting
7 Calibration Attendance & Alignment 100% / ≤5% Full attendance at all calibration sessions; scoring variance between the QA Assessor and the Lead should remain within 5 percentage points on calibrated calls. Prepare scored sample calls before each session; document disagreements and resolution; apply agreed interpretation updates to future scoring. Primary Owner
8 Reporting Timeliness 100% on-time Weekly QA reports submitted to the Quality & Training Lead by the agreed deadline each week, with zero missing agent entries. Use the standard report template; complete evaluations daily rather than batching; flag data blockers 48 hours before deadline. Primary Owner

📝 Job Summary

The Quality & Training Lead is responsible for designing, implementing, and continuously improving the quality assurance framework and training ecosystem for the Tap2eat Customer Experience team. This role leads a team of Quality Assessors, owns the end-to-end QA process across all customer interaction channels, and drives strategic training programmes that build agent capability, improve key CX metrics, and deliver an exceptional service experience to parents and schools enrolled in the programme. The Lead translates quality data into actionable people-development strategies, champions a culture of continuous improvement, and serves as the primary quality and learning partner to CX leadership and the wider Operations division.

⚙️ Roles & Responsibilities

Quality Assurance Leadership

  • 1 Design, own, and continuously evolve the QA scorecard, quality standards, and evaluation criteria for all customer interaction channels — ensuring alignment with the Tap2eat programme's service vision and operational goals.
  • 2 Oversee and govern the daily and weekly QA evaluation process conducted by Quality Assessors; review scored interactions for calibration accuracy and consistency across the team.
  • 3 Lead all calibration sessions across QA Assessors, CX Team Leads, and the CX Manager; establish scoring norms, resolve disputes, and publish aligned interpretation guidelines.
  • 4 Analyse aggregate QA data to identify systemic quality trends, recurring agent error patterns, and channel-level performance gaps; develop targeted corrective action plans.
  • 5 Own the escalation pathway for critical quality risks including compliance concerns, data integrity breaches, or repeated service failures; brief Operations leadership with recommended actions.

Training & Development

  • 6 Design and deliver structured onboarding training programmes for new CX agents covering product knowledge, communication standards, CRM navigation, escalation protocols, and Tap2eat programme specifics.
  • 7 Identify skills gaps and training needs from QA data, customer feedback, and team performance metrics; develop and roll out targeted upskilling modules and refresher programmes.
  • 8 Create, maintain, and update all training materials including SOPs, quick-reference guides, call scripts, FAQs, and e-learning content — ensuring materials remain current with programme changes.
  • 9 Track training completion, post-training performance improvement, and knowledge retention; measure training effectiveness using QA scores pre/post intervention and report findings to leadership.
  • 10 Manage nesting-period readiness assessments for new agents, certifying readiness to go live based on structured evaluation criteria, and providing structured recommendations to the CX Manager.

People Management & Reporting

  • 11 Line-manage Quality Assessors: set clear performance expectations, conduct regular one-on-ones, deliver performance reviews, provide professional development coaching, and manage attendance and accountability.
  • 12 Produce and present weekly, monthly, and quarterly CX quality and training performance reports to the Technical Operations Manager and CX Manager, including trend analysis, intervention results, and forward recommendations.
  • 13 Provide the People team with training and quality insights to support probation decisions, promotions, disciplinary processes, and headcount planning for the CX function.
  • 14 Champion a culture of quality, accountability, and continuous learning within the CX team; model best-practice call handling and customer communication standards at all times.
  • 15 Partner with the CRM and IT teams to ensure the quality monitoring toolset — call recording, QA management platform, and CRM — remains fit for purpose; flag capability gaps and propose enhancements.

🎯 Quality & Training Lead — KPI Framework
# KPI Target Description & Why It Matters How You Own It Ownership
1 Team QA Score (Overall) ≥ 95% The aggregate QA score across all agents for the period. Reflects the Lead's ability to set standards, build assessor consistency, and drive improvement through coaching and training interventions. Run weekly reviews, lead calibration sessions, deploy targeted training when scores dip. Own the corrective action plan. Primary Owner
2 First Call Resolution (FCR) ≥ 95% Percentage of parent/school issues resolved on first contact, at team level. The Lead is accountable for building the SOPs, call-flow guides, and escalation frameworks that make FCR achievable. Design clear escalation pathways, train on issue ownership, monitor repeat contacts weekly and address root causes in training. Primary Owner
3 Average Handle Time (Team) ≤ 180 sec Team-level AHT. The Lead owns the training content — quick-reference FAQs, CRM navigation SOPs, and call management guides — that enables agents to handle contacts efficiently without sacrificing quality. Develop and update quick-reference guides; run AHT coaching clinics; review outlier recordings and redesign content as needed. Primary Owner
4 Training Completion Rate ≥ 95% Percentage of agents who complete all assigned training modules (onboarding, refresher, upskilling) within the stipulated timeframe. Ensures the entire team operates with current product and process knowledge. Maintain a training calendar; send reminders; partner with the CX Team Lead on scheduling; report incomplete completions with cause. Primary Owner
5 Post-Training Score Improvement ≥ 10%pts Average QA score improvement for agents who received targeted training interventions, measured 30 days post-training against the 30-day pre-training baseline. Validates whether training is working. Always record pre-training QA baseline; design assessments tied to learning objectives; run 30-day post-training QA deep-dives. Primary Owner
6 New Agent Onboarding Readiness 100% certified Every new agent achieves the minimum certification score on the nesting readiness assessment before going fully live. No agent goes unsupervised without a documented sign-off from the Quality & Training Lead. Own the readiness scorecard; personally review nesting-period QA evaluations; provide structured certification reports to the CX Manager. Primary Owner
7 CRM Data Accuracy (Team) ≥ 98% Percentage of CRM records that are complete and accurate at team level. The Lead owns the training and standard that produces accurate data entry; QA Assessors score it per interaction. Embed CRM accuracy in the QA scorecard; run weekly audits; train on correct data capture; escalate systemic issues to the CRM team. Primary Owner
8 QA Assessor Performance Score ≥ 90% Composite score of QA Assessors' own performance against their KPIs — monitoring coverage, calibration alignment, coaching completion, and reporting timeliness. Reflects the Lead's effectiveness in managing the QA team. Conduct monthly performance reviews with each assessor; track their KPIs weekly; provide coaching and development support proactively. Primary Owner
9 QA Reporting Delivery 100% on-time Weekly, monthly, and quarterly quality and training performance reports submitted to the Technical Operations Manager and CX Manager on the agreed schedule, complete and with actionable recommendations. Maintain a reporting calendar; use standardised templates; build dashboards where possible to reduce manual effort and ensure accuracy. Primary Owner
10 SOP / Training Material Currency 100% current Percentage of SOPs, quick-reference guides, and training documents that are reviewed and updated within 10 working days of any programme change, product update, or process amendment. Prevents agents from operating on outdated information. Subscribe to all programme change communications; maintain a document version log; audit material currency monthly and report gaps. Primary Owner
Legend: Primary Owner = solely accountable for hitting and owning this metric   Supporting = contributes to but reports to someone else
🌽
Leadership Note
As Quality & Training Lead, you no longer just monitor quality — you create the conditions for quality to exist. Your KPIs reflect accountability for the system, the people, and the results. The Quality Assessors you manage own the day-to-day evaluation; your job is to ensure they have the right tools, the right training, the right feedback, and the right standards to succeed. When the team wins on quality, that is your success to own.
