SOP 01 · Customer Experience
Inbound Call Handling
The standard procedure every agent must follow from the moment an inbound call connects to the moment the CRM record is closed. No step is optional.

📋 Step-by-Step Call Procedure
  • 1
    Pre-Call Readiness
    Before accepting a call, ensure your CRM is open and logged in, your headset is working, and you are at your workstation ready to type. Do not accept a call if you are mid-task on a previous CRM record. Complete wrap-up before going available.
  • 2
    Standard Opening
    Answer within 3 rings. Use the approved greeting — include the time of day, programme name, your name, and an offer to assist. Do not deviate from the opening structure.
    "Good morning/afternoon, thank you for calling Tap2Eat. My name is [Name] — how may I assist you today?"
  • 3
    Caller Verification
    Before accessing or discussing any account information, verify the caller's identity using two of the following: registered phone number, child's name, school name, or account PIN. Log the verification method used in CRM. Never skip verification — this is a critical compliance item.
    "To protect your account, could I please confirm your [registered phone number / child's name]?"
  • 4
    Active Listening & Issue Identification
    Allow the caller to explain their issue without interruption. Use active listening cues ("I understand", "I hear you"). Do not begin troubleshooting until you have clearly identified the root issue. Repeat the issue back to confirm understanding before proceeding.
    "Just to confirm I understand — you're reaching out about [restate issue]. Is that correct?"
  • 5
    Open CRM & Review Account
    Search the parent's account in CRM while the caller is still talking. Review account status, balance, school registration, and previous interaction history before offering any resolution. Never offer a resolution based on assumptions — always verify in CRM first.
  • 6
    Hold Procedure (If Required)
    If you need to consult a resource or colleague, ask permission before placing on hold. State the reason and estimated wait time (maximum 60 seconds). Return before the estimated time if possible. Always thank the caller for holding.
    "I'd like to look into this for you — would you mind holding for approximately one minute while I check?" ... "Thank you for holding, [Name]. I have the information you need."
  • 7
    Resolution Delivery
    Provide a clear, accurate, and complete resolution. Use simple language — avoid technical jargon. If the resolution involves action on the account, state what you are doing and what the outcome will be. Confirm the caller understands the resolution before closing.
  • 8
    Escalation Decision
    Escalate to the Team Lead only when: the issue involves a system error, safeguarding concern, legal complaint, refund above your authorisation, or unresolved issue after 2 attempts. Do not escalate for emotional tone alone — de-escalate first. If escalating, use a warm transfer (never cold-drop the caller).
  • 9
    Closing Procedure
    Before ending the call, confirm the issue has been resolved, offer further assistance, and close warmly. Never end a call abruptly. Use the approved closing script.
    "Is there anything else I can help you with today? ... Thank you for calling Tap2Eat, [Name]. Have a lovely day."
  • 10
    Post-Call CRM Wrap-Up
    Log the interaction in CRM immediately — before accepting another call. The log must include: contact reason, actions taken, resolution provided, and case status (resolved/escalated/pending). Incomplete or missing logs are a CRM accuracy failure and will be flagged in your QA evaluation.

✓ Always Do
Verify before discussing any account details
Empathise before problem-solving
Confirm the caller's understanding of the resolution
Log CRM immediately after every call
Use the caller's name throughout the call
✗ Never Do
Discuss account details without verification
Place on hold without asking permission
End a call without confirming resolution
Offer a resolution without checking CRM first
Delay CRM wrap-up to accept another call
SOP 02 · Customer Experience
Outbound Call Handling
Procedure for all agent-initiated calls — follow-ups, callbacks, and proactive parent outreach. Outbound calls carry the same quality standards as inbound.
AHT Target: ≤ 180 seconds Max Dial Attempts: 3 Channel: Outbound Voice
📋 Step-by-Step Outbound Procedure
  • 1
    Pre-Call Preparation
    Before dialling, open the parent's CRM record and review: the reason for the callback, previous interaction notes, account status, and any open cases. Know the purpose of the call before dialling. Do not make an outbound call without reviewing the account first.
  • 2
    Dial Attempt & Voicemail
    Dial the registered number. If unanswered, wait for at least 8 rings before ending. Do not leave voicemail unless specifically instructed. Log the attempt in CRM with date and time. Maximum 3 dial attempts on the same day — space them at least 30 minutes apart. After 3 failed attempts, log as "Unreachable" and escalate to Team Lead for next steps.
  • 3
    Outbound Opening & Verification
    When connected, identify yourself and Tap2Eat before asking for any confirmation. Verify you are speaking with the correct person before stating the purpose of the call.
    "Good morning/afternoon, my name is [Name] calling from Tap2Eat. Am I speaking with [Parent Name]? ... Thank you — I'm calling regarding [brief reason]. Is now a good time to talk?"
  • 4
    Call Purpose & Resolution
    State the purpose clearly within the first 30 seconds. Provide the resolution, update, or information as prepared. Keep the call focused — outbound calls should be concise. Follow the same resolution quality standards as inbound: clear, accurate, confirmed by the parent.
  • 5
    Handling Objections or New Issues
    If the parent raises a new issue during the outbound call, use your judgement: if it is quick and straightforward, resolve it now. If it requires investigation, book a callback and log the new issue in CRM. Do not let a new issue extend the call beyond 180 seconds without cause.
  • 6
    Close & CRM Log
    Confirm the purpose of the call has been addressed, offer further assistance, and close professionally. Log the outcome in CRM immediately: reason for call, what was discussed, resolution, and case status.
    "Is there anything else I can help you with today? ... Thank you for your time, [Name]. Have a great day."
⚠️
Data Privacy on Outbound Calls: If the person who answers is not the registered parent, do not disclose any account or programme information. State you will call back and log the attempt. Never leave account details with a third party.
SOP 03 · Customer Experience
Chat Handling — WhatsApp & SMS
Written channel communications require a different approach to voice — precision, clarity, and tone are everything when there is no vocal delivery to support the message.
Response Time: ≤ 30 minutes (business hours) QA Score Target: ≥ 98% Channels: WhatsApp · SMS
📋 Chat Handling Procedure
  • 1
    Acknowledge Within 30 Minutes
    All incoming WhatsApp or SMS messages must receive an acknowledgement within 30 minutes during business hours (8am–5pm, Monday–Friday). If a full resolution is not immediately possible, send an acknowledgement confirming you have received the message and will respond shortly.
    "Hello [Name], thank you for contacting Tap2Eat. I've received your message and am looking into this for you now — I'll follow up shortly."
  • 2
    Verify Identity Before Discussing Account
    Request verification via chat before discussing any account details. Ask the parent to confirm their registered phone number or child's full name. Log the verification method in CRM.
    "To help you securely, could you please confirm the phone number registered on your Tap2Eat account?"
  • 3
    Tone & Language Standards
    Written tone must be professional but warm. Use the parent's name. Write in complete sentences. Avoid abbreviations (e.g. "pls", "asap", "u"). Avoid ALL CAPS. Use emojis sparingly — only when they add warmth, never more than one per message. If the parent writes in Kiswahili, respond in Kiswahili or bilingual.
  • 4
    Resolution via Chat
    Resolve the issue fully in chat if possible. Provide clear, concise information in one message where possible — avoid sending multiple fragmented messages. If the issue requires a phone call, offer to call the parent and confirm a time.
    "I can see the issue on your account. [Explanation of resolution]. This should be resolved within [timeframe]. Please let me know if you have any further questions."
  • 5
    Escalation from Chat
    If the issue cannot be resolved via chat, escalate in one of two ways: (1) Offer a callback — confirm the parent's number and preferred time. (2) Escalate to the Team Lead for complex issues. Log the escalation reason in CRM. Do not leave a chat thread without a resolution or a clear next step communicated to the parent.
  • 6
    Closing the Conversation
    Once the issue is resolved, confirm resolution in the chat, invite the parent to return if they need further help, and close warmly. Log the complete interaction in CRM — including the platform (WhatsApp/SMS), issue, and outcome.
    "I'm glad we could sort that out for you, [Name]. Don't hesitate to reach out if you need anything else. Have a great day! 😊"

Always close with a next step or confirmed resolution
✗ Never Do

SOP 04 · Customer Experience
Email Handling
Email responses require the highest level of written professionalism. Emails are formal records — they may be forwarded, quoted, or used in disputes. Every word matters.
Response Time: ≤ 24 hours (business hours) Tone: Formal but warm Channel: Email
📋 Email Response Procedure
  • 1
    Triage & Response Time
    All emails must be acknowledged within 24 business hours. Urgent emails (payment failures, complaints, account suspension) are prioritised and must be responded to within 4 hours. Flag any email involving a legal threat, media, or data concern to the Team Lead immediately — do not respond to these without approval.
  • 2
    Email Structure
    Every email response must contain: a personalised greeting with the parent's name, a reference to what they contacted us about, the resolution or next steps, a closing offer of further assistance, and a professional sign-off with your name, role, and contact channel.
    "Dear [Parent Name],

    Thank you for reaching out to Tap2Eat. I am writing in response to your enquiry regarding [subject].

    [Resolution/explanation].

    Please do not hesitate to contact us if you need any further assistance. We are available on [phone/WhatsApp] during business hours.

    Warm regards,
    [Your Name] | Tap2Eat Customer Experience Team"
  • 3
    Verification via Email
    If an email requires account-specific information and the identity is unclear, request verification before sharing details. Ask the parent to confirm their registered phone number or child's school by replying to the email. Log this in CRM.
  • 4
    Tone & Proofreading
    Always proofread before sending. Check spelling, grammar, and that the parent's name is spelled correctly throughout. Avoid casual language. Never use emojis in emails. Do not use bullet points excessively — write in clear paragraphs. If in doubt about the tone, read it aloud before sending.
  • 5
    CRM Logging
    Every email interaction must be logged in CRM: the date, channel (email), issue category, summary of response, and case status. Paste or summarise the key points of both the inbound email and your response. Do not log "email sent" without a summary.
🚫
Never respond to emails requesting sensitive financial data, legal action threats, or media enquiries without Team Lead approval. Forward these immediately and do not draft a response yourself.
SOP 05 · Data Integrity
CRM Data Entry & Accuracy
Every record you create or update feeds the reports and decisions that run this programme. Inaccurate data causes payment failures, missed communications, and poor service. Data accuracy is not optional — it is a KPI.
CRM Accuracy Target: ≥ 98% Log Every Interaction No Delayed Wrap-Ups
📋 Required Fields — Every Interaction
  • 1
    Parent / Account Details
    Verify and confirm: full parent name (no abbreviations), registered phone number (include country code: +254), child's full name, school name (exact as registered). If any of these details are incorrect or outdated, update them during the interaction and note the change.
  • 2
    Interaction Log — Required Fields
    Every CRM log must contain: Date and time, Channel (inbound call / outbound call / WhatsApp / SMS / email), Contact reason (use the correct category from the dropdown), Summary of what was discussed and what action was taken, Resolution status (Resolved / Escalated / Pending callback / Unresolved), Agent name (auto-populated — verify it is correct).
  • 3
    Case Categorisation
    Always select the correct contact reason category. Do not use "Other" unless no category exists. If "Other" is selected, you must write a full description — it cannot be left blank. Incorrect categorisation skews reporting data and makes quality analysis inaccurate.
  • 4
    Timing — Log Before Next Interaction
    CRM wrap-up must be completed before accepting another call or responding to another chat. Batching logs at the end of a shift is a data integrity failure. Real-time logging ensures nothing is missed and details are accurate.
  • 5
    Corrections & Amendments
    If you discover an error in a CRM record, correct it immediately. Add an amendment note stating what was changed and when. Do not delete records. Do not create duplicate entries — search thoroughly before creating a new record. Report all duplicate accounts to the QA Assessor for cleanup.
🔍 CRM Audit — What the QA Assessor Checks Weekly
📊
The QA Assessor runs a weekly spot-check on 10–15 CRM records per agent. They check: all required fields are populated, the contact reason is correctly categorised, the case status matches what actually happened, the log was completed within the interaction wrap-up window, and no duplicate records exist for the same parent. A CRM score below 95% on any given week triggers a coaching session.
SOP 06 · Escalation Management

Escalation is not a failure — but unnecessary escalation is. This SOP tells you exactly when to escalate, who to escalate to, and how to hand over without losing the parent.

🔄 Warm Transfer Procedure
  • 1
    Inform the Parent
    Tell the parent you are transferring them and why. Give them the name of the person they will speak to. Never cold-transfer — always brief the receiving agent before connecting.
    "I'm going to connect you with our Team Lead, [Name], who will be best placed to assist with this. Please hold for just a moment while I brief them."
  • 2
    Brief the Receiving Agent
    Before connecting the parent, brief the Team Lead in full: parent's name, account number, issue summary, what you have already tried, and why you are escalating. This prevents the parent from having to repeat themselves.
  • 3
    Introduce the Transfer
    Reconnect with the parent and make the introduction before stepping off the call.
    "[Parent Name], I have [Team Lead Name] on the line who has all the details and will take great care of you from here."
  • 4
    Log in CRM Immediately
    Log the escalation in CRM before doing anything else: reason, what was tried, who it was escalated to, and the time. Mark the case status as "Escalated". The Team Lead will update the case with the outcome.
