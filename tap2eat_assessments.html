Opening & Structure (Q1–4)
1 Which greeting correctly follows the Tap2Eat standard opening procedure?
A"Hello, how can I help?"
B"Good morning/afternoon, thank you for calling Tap2Eat. My name is [Name] — how may I assist you today?"

D"Hi this is [Name], what's your problem?"
B is correct. The opening must include: time-of-day greeting, programme name, your name, and an offer to assist — in that order.
2 An angry parent starts shouting. The correct approach is:
ARaise your voice to match their urgency
BPlace them on hold until they calm down
CStay calm, lower your tone, acknowledge their frustration, then move to resolution
DWarn them you will end the call if they continue
C is correct. De-escalation requires a calm, lower tone. Acknowledge the emotion before solving the problem.
3 You should place a parent on hold when:
AYou need a moment to think
BYou need to check a resource — after asking permission and giving an estimated wait time
CThe call is approaching 180 seconds
DThe parent sounds confused
B is correct. Holds require: reason + parent's permission + estimated wait time. Never hold to manage AHT.
4 Which is an example of an empathetic statement?
A"That's not our fault."
B"I understand your issue but the system shows otherwise."
C"I completely understand how frustrating that must be — let me look into this for you right now."
D"You should have checked your account earlier."
C is correct. Empathy validates the feeling before jumping to the solution. "Right now" signals urgency and care.
