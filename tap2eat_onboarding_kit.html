🌱
The nesting period is not a formality. It is the bridge between training and live calls. No intern goes fully live until the Quality & Training Lead has signed off on their readiness. Use this time to ask questions — it costs nothing here, but costs a lot once you're live.
Phase 1 · Days 6–8
Silent Shadowing

Sit alongside a senior agent. Listen to live calls with your headset in. Do not speak. Observe everything — how they open, how they use the CRM in real time, how they handle frustrated parents, how they wrap up.

  • Minimum 3 shadow sessions with different agents
  • Complete shadow observation form after each session
  • Note at least 3 questions to bring to debrief
  • Q&T Lead reviews shadow forms end of Phase 1
Phase 2 · Days 9–13
Supervised Live Calls

Take live calls with your supervisor listening silently. Handle the interaction fully. After each call, receive immediate verbal feedback. QA Assessor scores a selection of these calls using the full scorecard.

  • Minimum 15 supervised calls completed
  • Same-day verbal feedback after every session
  • QA scores reviewed with you every 2 days
  • Target score during Phase 2 is ≥ 75%
  • Any score below 60% triggers a coaching hold
Phase 3 · Days 14–15
Readiness Certification

Final assessment by the Quality & Training Lead. Includes one observed live call session plus a short competency check. Pass criteria must be met before going fully independent.

  • Final QA score ≥ 80% to pass
  • CRM accuracy ≥ 95% on assessed calls
  • Escalation decision correct on all test scenarios
  • Q&T Lead issues written readiness sign-off
  • Failed phase = extend by 5 days with support plan

🏅
What Readiness Certification Means
Passing the nesting period does not mean you are perfect — it means you have demonstrated that you can handle a live Tap2eat parent interaction safely, professionally, and with the right instincts. Ongoing QA evaluations, coaching, and training continue throughout your internship. Certification is the beginning, not the finish line.
📋 Assessment Instructions
Pass mark: 80% (16/20 questions correct). You must complete this assessment individually without referring to your notes. You will have 30 minutes. If you score below 80%, you will receive targeted coaching and one resit attempt within 2 working days. Press "Show Answer Key" at the bottom to reveal correct answers after completing the assessment.
Section A — Programme Knowledge (5 questions)
1 What is the primary purpose of the Tap2eat programme?
ATo provide free internet access to schools in Kenya
BTo enable parents and schools to manage school meal payments and delivery through the Food for Education platform
CTo deliver food directly to parents' homes on subscription
DTo manage teacher payroll for partner schools
B is correct. Tap2eat is the parent-facing service of Food for Education, enabling payment, registration, and meal management for enrolled schools.
2 A parent calls and says their child didn't receive a meal today despite having a positive balance. What is your FIRST action?
AImmediately escalate to the Team Lead
BEmpathise, pull up the parent's CRM record, and verify account status and meal schedule
CTell the parent the school is responsible and give them the school's number
DAsk the parent to send a WhatsApp message instead
B is correct. Always empathise first, then investigate using the CRM before deciding whether to escalate or resolve directly. Escalating without checking the record wastes everyone's time.
3 Which of the following is an example of a CORRECT CRM log entry?
A"Parent called about lunch"
B"Rude parent, didn't help"
C"Parent enquired re: meal non-delivery on 14/05. Verified account balance (KES 450). Confirmed delivery log shows meal recorded. Advised parent to confirm with class teacher. Closed — no further action required."
D"Called back but no answer"
C is correct. A proper CRM entry includes: the contact reason, what was checked, what was found, what action was taken, and how the case was closed.
4 How long should a standard call handle time (AHT) be at most?
A60 seconds
B120 seconds
C180 seconds
D300 seconds
C is correct. The AHT target is ≤180 seconds (3 minutes). This includes talk time and any immediate wrap-up/CRM entry after the call.
5 If a parent contacts you a second time about the same unresolved issue, this is recorded as a failure of which KPI?
AFirst Call Resolution (FCR)
BQuality Assurance Score
CAverage Handle Time
DCRM Data Accuracy
A is correct. First Call Resolution measures whether an issue is resolved on the first contact. A repeat contact for the same issue counts as a failed FCR instance.
Section B — Call Standards (5 questions)
6 Which greeting correctly follows the Tap2eat call opening standard?
A"Hello, how can I help you today?"
B"Good morning/afternoon, thank you for calling Tap2eat. My name is [Name] — how may I assist you today?"
C"Tap2eat customer service, speak."
D"Hi, this is [Name], what's your problem?"
B is correct. A proper opening includes a time-of-day greeting, the programme name, your name, and an offer to assist — in that order.
7 A parent becomes very angry and starts shouting. What is the correct approach?
ARaise your voice slightly to match their urgency so they know you are serious
BPlace them on hold immediately until they calm down
CStay calm, lower your tone, acknowledge their frustration, and say "I hear you — let me look into this for you right now"
DWarn the parent that you will end the call if they continue shouting
C is correct. De-escalation requires a calm, lower tone — never matching the parent's energy. Acknowledging the frustration before problem-solving is a key empathy technique.
8 When is it correct to place a parent on hold?
AWhenever you need a moment to think
BWhen you need to consult a resource or colleague and cannot resolve the query in real time — only after asking permission and informing the parent of the expected wait time
CWhen the parent sounds confused and you need to read the SOP
DWhen calls are running too long and AHT is rising
B is correct. Holds require: (1) a reason, (2) permission from the parent, (3) an estimated wait time. Never hold without notifying. Never hold to manage AHT.
9 A parent asks you to process a refund of KES 2,000. You are not authorised to do this. What do you do?
ATell the parent refunds are not available
BProcess it anyway to keep the parent happy
CAcknowledge the request, explain you'll need to escalate to the Team Lead, take ownership of the follow-up, and give a clear timeframe
DAsk the parent to email the refund request
C is correct. When you cannot resolve an issue yourself, you must: acknowledge, explain, escalate with ownership (not pass the parent off), and set a clear expectation.
10 Which of these is an example of an empathetic statement?
A"That's not our fault."
B"I understand your issue but our system is correct."
C"I completely understand how frustrating that must be — let me look into this for you right now."
D"You should have checked your account earlier."
C is correct. Empathy means validating the parent's feeling before jumping to the solution. Phrases like "I understand" and "right now" signal urgency and care simultaneously.
Section C — Scenarios & Escalations (5 questions)
11 A parent reports that their child has been eating meals for two weeks but the balance hasn't been deducted. What should you do FIRST?
APull up the CRM record, check transaction history and meal logs, verify whether it's a billing delay or a system error, and escalate if confirmed as a system issue
BTell the parent to contact their school's finance office
CInform the parent that the balance will be deducted retroactively soon
DAsk the parent to stop allowing their child to eat until it is resolved
A is correct. Always investigate in the CRM first. Billing anomalies require verification before any explanation is given to the parent to avoid misinformation.
12 When should you escalate to the Team Lead WITHOUT first attempting to resolve?
AWhen the parent sounds upset
BWhen the call is running past 180 seconds
CWhen the issue involves a system error, safeguarding concern, legal threat, or refund above your authorisation limit
DWhen you are unsure what to say next
C is correct. Immediate escalation is required for: system errors, safeguarding, legal threats, and transactions above your authorisation level. Emotional tone or uncertainty is not a reason to escalate.
13 A parent sends a WhatsApp message in Kiswahili. You are comfortable in English. What should you do?
AReply in English only
BIgnore the message until a Kiswahili-speaking agent is available
CRespond in both English and Kiswahili where possible, or flag to a bilingual colleague while keeping the parent informed of the handover
DAsk the parent to resend their message in English
C is correct. Language should never be a barrier. Make every effort to communicate in the parent's preferred language. Handovers to bilingual colleagues are acceptable with proper notification to the parent.
14 After resolving a call, what must you do before moving to the next interaction?
ATake a short break to recover
BComplete the CRM log — recording the contact reason, actions taken, resolution, and case status — before accepting the next interaction
CCheck WhatsApp for new messages
DReport the call outcome verbally to your Team Lead
B is correct. Every interaction must be logged in CRM immediately after the call — before the next contact. Delayed or missed logs directly impact CRM Data Accuracy KPI.
15 You accidentally log incorrect school details for a parent's account. What should you do?
ALeave it and hope no one notices
BDelete the record entirely and start a new one
CCorrect the record immediately in CRM, add a note explaining the amendment and when it was made, and inform your supervisor
DAsk IT to fix it at the end of the week
C is correct. Errors must be corrected immediately, documented with an amendment note, and disclosed to your supervisor. Data integrity is non-negotiable.
Section D — Quality & Standards (5 questions)
16 What is the minimum QA score you must maintain as a certified agent?
A70%
B80%
C95%
D100%
C is correct. The QA Score target is ≥95%. Scores below 90% trigger a mandatory coaching session within 5 working days.
17 Which of these is NOT a quality parameter assessed on the QA scorecard?
AProfessionalism and tone
BFirst Call Resolution
CNumber of calls taken per hour
DCRM accuracy
C is correct. Volume metrics (calls per hour) are not on the QA scorecard. QA measures quality of interaction — not quantity. Professionalism, FCR, empathy, and CRM accuracy are all scored.
18 A Quality Assessor gives you a score of 82% and feedback you disagree with. What should you do?
AIgnore the feedback — it was a one-off
BComplain to the CX Manager directly
CRaise a formal dispute with the Quality Assessor within 24 hours, referencing specific scorecard items, and request a review by the Quality & Training Lead
DAsk a senior colleague to speak to the assessor on your behalf
C is correct. Disputes must be raised directly and formally within 24 hours, citing specific scorecard criteria. The Q&T Lead is the final arbitrator for all score disputes.
19 What does "calibration" mean in a call centre QA context?
AAdjusting your headset audio levels at the start of a shift
BA session where QA Assessors and supervisors score the same call independently, then compare scores to ensure consistency in how the scorecard is applied
CTraining new agents how to use the CRM correctly
DSetting performance targets for the coming month
B is correct. Calibration ensures that all QA Assessors interpret the scorecard the same way — so every agent is evaluated fairly regardless of who scores their call.
20 Why is a QA score below 90% on a single interaction not necessarily catastrophic?
ABecause QA scores don't really matter for interns
BBecause the scorecard has known errors
CBecause QA is evaluated on trends over multiple interactions — one score triggers a coaching conversation to prevent the pattern, not an immediate disciplinary action
DBecause interns are not expected to reach 90% for the first 6 months
C is correct. A single low score is a learning signal, not a punishment trigger. The coaching conversation that follows is designed to prevent it becoming a trend. Consistent sub-90% scores over a period are the concern.

The practical assessment is the final gate before certification. It uses real Tap2eat scenarios on a live or simulated call. The Q&T Lead scores every dimension using the same rubric applied to live agents — because you will be held to the same standard once certified.
🎯 Practical Assessment — Scoring Rubric
Dimension Exceeds (5) Meets (4) Below (2–3) Fails (0–1)
Opening & Greeting Warm, complete, brand-accurate, confident All elements present, minor tone gap Missing 1 element or slightly robotic Missing 2+ elements or incorrect opening
Empathy & Tone Natural, specific, validates feeling before solution Empathises but generic phrasing Empathy absent on one occasion or sounds scripted Dismissive, cold, or impatient at any point
Issue Identification Quickly and accurately identifies root cause; clarifies without interrogating Identifies issue correctly with 1–2 clarifying questions Takes longer than necessary; minor misidentification Wrong issue identified or no attempt to clarify
CRM Navigation & Log Navigates CRM fluently; complete, accurate, immediate log Accurate log, minor delay or brief navigation pause Log incomplete or delayed; one data field inaccurate No log created, wrong record updated, or major inaccuracy
Resolution & FCR Issue fully resolved or escalated correctly with ownership and ETA Issue resolved with minor clarification gap Partial resolution; follow-up left unclear Issue unresolved, incorrect advice, or parent not informed
Escalation Judgement Correct escalation decision every time; warm transfer with full handover brief Correct decision; handover brief slightly incomplete Escalated when unnecessary or slight delay in correct decision Failed to escalate when required or escalated a simple query
Handle Time (AHT) Completed within 150 seconds without sacrificing quality 151–180 seconds 181–240 seconds >240 seconds or rushed to the point of error
Closing & Wrap-Up Confirms resolution, offers further help, closes warmly, logs immediately Closes correctly with all required elements Missing one closing element (e.g. no confirmation of resolution) Abrupt end, no confirmation, no log after call

Quality & Training Lead
A day that builds quality across every level.
No two days are identical — but the rhythm is consistent. You move between evaluation, coaching, training delivery, data analysis, and leadership. Here's what a typical Wednesday looks like.

🔍 QA & Evaluation 📚 Training & Coaching 📊 Data & Reporting 👥 People Management 📋 Admin & Planning 🎯 Leadership & Comms
📅 Wednesday — Full Day Schedule
7:30
30 min
Morning prep & floor readiness check
Arrive before the floor opens. Check the previous day's QA dashboard for any overnight flags. Confirm the evaluation queue is loaded for the day. Review attendance — are all agents in? Is there a nesting-period intern who needs a supervisor on-seat today? Brief the Team Lead on anything that needs floor-level attention before the first call drops.
🔍 QA Review🎯 Floor Leadership
8:00
45 min
Live QA monitoring — morning batch
Pull up the call recording system and monitor 4–6 live or recent recordings from agents flagged by the QA Assessor in yesterday's shift. Focus especially on any agent who dipped below 90% last week. Score each one on the QA scorecard. Annotate specific moments for coaching feedback — exact timestamps, exact phrasing, what was missing and why it matters.
🔍 QA Evaluation
8:45
30 min
QA Assessor daily brief & task assignment
10-minute stand-up with the Quality Assessors. Share any trends spotted from the morning monitoring. Assign today's evaluation targets by agent name — who each assessor monitors, what channels to focus on, and any priority call types (e.g. payment complaints after a system maintenance window). Check their coaching log status — any overdue feedback sessions from last week?
👥 Team Management🔍 QA Oversight
9:15
45 min
One-on-one coaching session — underperforming agent
Sit down with an agent who scored 84% on Tuesday. Bring the scorecard, the specific recordings, and a coaching plan. Walk through each missed point — play back the exact moment on the recording. This isn't a disciplinary meeting; it's a development conversation. By the end, the agent has 2–3 specific, actionable things to practise, and has signed the coaching record. Follow-up review call booked for next Tuesday.
📚 1-on-1 Coaching👥 People Dev
10:00
60 min
Training delivery — new intern group session
Module 2 trainer-led session for the current intake: Call Handling Standards. You deliver the session yourself — not just assigning it. Walk interns through the call structure, play model recordings, do paired role-plays with scenario cards, debrief as a group. Good training now means fewer QA failures later. End with a quick 5-question verbal check of comprehension. Note any intern who looks uncertain — schedule a follow-up before their nesting period begins.
📚 Training Facilitation
11:00
30 min
Nesting period supervision — intern on supervised calls
Sit in on two supervised live calls with the intern currently in nesting Phase 2. Silent observation. After each call, give immediate verbal feedback — what worked, one specific thing to adjust. Score the calls on the nesting rubric. Log the scores. If the pattern from yesterday continues (missing wrap-up confirmation), today is the day you make it the focused development point.
🔍 Nesting Evaluation📚 Coaching
11:30
30 min
🥦 Lunch break — you are not the floor. Disconnect, eat, reset.
12:00
90 min
Weekly QA data compilation & trend analysis
Pull all QA scores from the week so far. Build the mid-week snapshot: average QA score by agent, FCR rate by agent, AHT by agent, CRM accuracy audit results. Which agents are trending down? Which scenarios are generating the most failures? Is there a channel with disproportionate quality issues (e.g. WhatsApp scores lagging behind calls)? This analysis drives everything — what you coach, what you train, what you escalate to the Technical Operations Manager on Friday.
📊 Data Analysis📋 Reporting
13:30
45 min
Calibration session — weekly scorecard alignment
Bring the QA Assessors together. Each person has scored the same 3 calls independently before the session. Compare scores on screen. Discuss any divergence above 5 percentage points — what drove the different interpretation? Update the scoring notes document with agreed interpretations. This is not about who was right. It is about making sure every agent is scored fairly by any assessor on any day.
🔍 Calibration👥 Assessor Development

45 min
SOP & training material update
The CRM team pushed an update to the payment screen layout yesterday — agents are navigating it slightly differently now. Review the change, update the relevant quick-reference guide, send it to all agents via the group channel, and flag it for inclusion in next week's refresher session. This is unglamorous work that prevents quality failures. Outdated materials are a silent quality killer.
📋 SOP Management📚 Content Development
15:00
30 min
Quality Assessor 1-on-1 performance check-in
Monthly check-in with one of your QA Assessors. Review their own KPIs — monitoring coverage, coaching completion, calibration alignment. Are they hitting targets? If not, understand why — is it a capacity issue, a skills gap, or something else? Your assessors are your direct reports and your quality delivery team. If they're not performing well, your metrics suffer too. Invest in them as you would want your manager to invest in you.
👥 Performance Review

45 min
Afternoon QA monitoring batch & floor walk
Score 3 more calls — prioritise the afternoon shift agents you haven't covered yet this week. Then do a physical floor walk. Listen to tone. Watch CRM navigation. Is anyone looking stuck? Is there a call that's been running too long? Be visible. Your presence on the floor is itself a quality signal — agents who know the Q&T Lead might be listening perform to a higher standard. You're not there to catch people out; you're there to catch problems early.

Confirm the next intern group session is ready. Check if Friday's report to the Technical Operations Manager needs any data you haven't yet pulled. Leave the desk clear. A disorganised Quality Lead produces disorganised quality data.
